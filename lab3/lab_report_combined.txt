The sample mean (0.4903) is extremely close to the theoretical 
mean (0.5), showing that the expectation is the "long-run" average 
the random variable converges to over many trials. 

The variance tells us how much the data "spreads out" from the 
center. In the tight graph (Var: 0.0034), the numbers are 
squished near 0.5, while in the wide graph (Var: 0.0853), 
they cover the whole range. Higher variance simply means the 
data is more scattered and less predictable.
